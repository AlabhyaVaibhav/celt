Entrepreneurship Cell,KIIT (E-Cell)

The Entrepreneurship Cell, KIIT (E-Cell) firmly believes that emerging economies like India need the drive and innovation of young entrepreneurs. Founded in 2012, E-Cell not only motivates the youth toward entrepreneurship but also makes efforts to provide them with the necessary resources to do so. In order to achieve our vision of promoting the spirit of entrepreneurship among the youth, E-Cell has conceptualized initiatives for college students as well as young entrepreneurs and working professionals, leading to successful start-ups. KIIT E-Cell targets on fostering entrepreneurship culture amongst aspiring entrepreneurs with the East India's largest inbuilt technology business incubator KIIT TBI. This is achieved through a number of activities such as Discussion forums, Speaker sessions, B-plan competitions, summer internships and webinars by eminent international personalities which provide the tools to create an implementable plan from an idea. We deeply encourage the self-development of our members through the groundbreaking concept of E-School. Another initiative by KIIT E-Cell is the Entrepreneurship awareness camp, which is a three day workshop organized within the premises of the institution. It aims at developing entrepreneurial aptitude by bringing various recognized entrepreneurial leaders, E-cell mentors, faculty and the young minds by discussing pertinent topics and issues involved with entrepreneurial initiatives. In 2015, KIIT Entrepreneurship Cell hosted the Entrepreneurship Summit which turned out to be one of the finest business acquaintances to the youth with about 400 participants. The event comprised of various workshops on marketing-commerce, business process and development and many more in addition to the panel discussions, Hackathon, business quizzes and E-talks by best entrepreneurs and corporate people. Internship camp in Esummit aimed at providing summer internships including the virtual internship opportunities to the attendees in start-ups.

THE INTERNSHIP CAMP 2016
The Internship Camp, which was held from between 17th March 2016 to 20th March 2016, was an initiative of KIIT Entrepreneurship Cell with an aim to provide internship opportunities to undergraduate students from the field of technology, biotechnology, management, designing and law from Bhubaneswar and our home university. KIIT Entrepreneurship Cell takes pride to have provided this platform for the second time in a row. We managed to bring in around 60 companies and provide around 250 plus internship opportunities , thus turning out to be one of India’s largest such internship camp . The Internship Camp is supported by KIIT Technology Business Incubator, one of the largest incubation centres in Asia. This Internship Camp facilitates a symbiotic relationship between the firm and the students of KIIT University as well. In this camp we focus on providing the best set of talents to the companies keeping in mind their requirements . This unique platform ensures that the companies get the most young and creative brains who can add some value to their organization. Simultaneously the students also get to implement their knowledge in solving real life problems and gaining work experience. The internship camp also provides a networking platform to all the start-up and corporate firms to exchange ideas and increase their network.

E School
Entrepreneurship School or E School is one of a kind program in the country based on entrepreneurship. Never has been in any educational organization the concept of entrepreneurship ever introduced to the students in a structured curriculum. People say entrepreneurship cannot be taught through books. Even the top B Schools of India agree to this. We second that but in the current scenario the fast growing startup culture has made it relatively important to know the basics of the entrepreneurial world at an early stage. E School has been structured in a way most friendly to the students including how to begin with an idea and convert it into a proper company, how to approach the investors and business expansion.

Entrepreneurship Awareness Camp (EAC)
India is in a state of business and economic transformation. The number of young entrepreneurs is amassing day by day and the time is right to make the people and most importantly the students more and more aware and conversant of the concept of entrepreneurship. E Cell in association with the KIIT Technology Business Incubator (KIIT TBI) organizes a three day camp on a regular basis with speakers coming in from various domains

Start-Up Outreach The Start-Up Outreach programme is aimed at helping and mentoring existing start-ups as well as new ideas which need guidance to start-up. This programme provides great opportunities and motivation to all those young and budding minds who are new into the field of entrepreneurship. E-Cell provides constant mentoring and help with the expansion of business through providing them with contacts and connecting them to investors from all across the country.

E Chai
E-Chai is a platform for the young and innovative minds to interact with the budding entrepreneurs of our generation. Inspired by the IIMs concept of E-Chai the KIIT E-Cell decided to organise this event for the first time on 6 December, 2014 where some of the inspiring entrepreneurs form the city participated to discuss some of the basic problems in the entrepreneurial sector. The best part about the concept of E-Chai is that everyone is considered equal and no hierarchy is maintained. So from the CEO of a million dollar company to a local vendor all are same.
The last edition of the E-Chai saw some of the emerging entrepreneurs of the city including Mr Dina Krishna Mishra, founder INSYST an IT firm. Mr Ranjit Mohanty, owner and founder of an animation studio PACARD followed by Mr Shruti Kant Mishra, Chairman of MAIESTAS Group of Companies an electronic manufacturing firm. The invitees also included the esteemed mentors of KIIT E-Cell, Dr Mrityunjay Suar founder of KIIT Technology Business Incubator (TBI) and KIIT School of Biotechnology. Er. Devesh Rath, KIIT TBI and Prof. Kumar Mohanty, Dean CAAS KIIT University.
